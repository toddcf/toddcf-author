Catch Up To Myself

Synopsis

Lem Andrews used to feel like he was on top of Pacific Point University’s social pyramid. Now he’s scraping himself up off the bottom after two bad breakups leave him struggling.

Getting a girlfriend seems like the obvious answer to Lem’s problems, and there are plenty to choose from:

  • Mercedes is suggestive and flirtatious — maybe she wants to leave her abusive boyfriend for Lem.

  • Safrina is quick to smile and laugh when Lem’s around, and she’s bored with her long-distance boyfriend.

  • Monica is unobtainable — she even has a “no-boyfriend” policy. But maybe Lem’s the only one she would trust enough to let in.

  • And then there’s Mary, with her unrequited infatuation for Lem. Should he give her a chance?

They’re all around him; he just doesn’t know how to connect.

For that matter, Lem is now learning the hard way how little sympathy his friends have for any sign of weakness. If the girls and the morally-bankrupt friends won’t be the death of him, his own overly analytical mind will be. If he can’t master the ills that are plaguing him now, he’s going to be alone forever.

But Lem is determined to get a handle on the one person he can control — himself. Even if it means doing a hard reset.
