The Music of

— Catch Up To Myself —

Here are many of the songs I listened to while writing Catch Up To Myself (plus some I discovered after the fact, but which still fit the vibe). For me, they capture the various emotions that Lem experiences during his coming of age journey.

Army of Anyone

Richard Patrick from Filter, plus members of Stone Temple Pilots.

More on Filter later . . .

Army of Anyone

View on Amazon

The only album this supergroup ever released.

“A Better Place”

Contains the amazing lyric, “I wish you’d come in, but the place is blown apart.”

“This Wasn’t Supposed to Happen”

The Catherine Wheel

Frontman Rob Dickinson later went solo, and those songs will appear later on this list, as well.

These tracks from The Catherine Wheel’s first album are positively magical.

“Texture”

A very romantic, summery sound.

“I Want to Touch You”

“Black Metallic”

I’ve heard this song described as . . .

  • An “android love song.”

  • A love song about a car. (Not a bad guess, considering lead singer Rob Dickinson is obsessed with cars, and now runs an after-market Porsche customization shop in Southern California.)

  • A song about loving a woman who is growing more and more distant. (Rob himself supposedly mentioned this in an interview.)

Whatever it’s about lyrically, musically it is one of the most dreamy and romantic songs I have ever heard.

“Flower to Hide”

This is the album I had in mind when Lem puts in his earbuds and goes running on the beach, listening to the new album his friends just finished recording.

“Kill Rhythm”

In fact, this is the opening track I had in mind for that moment. From the novel:

The opening riffs of the album exploded into an expanse of sound as I hit the beach running, bringing back a small piece of my mushroom high. The amount of detail in each song was astounding.

“I Confess”

“Crank”

“Broken Head”

“Pain”

This is sort of crazy, but I originally thought the lyrics were, “Before the Summer Faire, I already knew.” And that one line bloomed in my mind, ultimately inspiring the entire Summer Faire sequence in Catch Up To Myself.

It wasn’t until years later that I discovered that the actual lyrics are something else, but I like my original misinterpration better.

“Strange Fruit”

Has that summer feeling of freedom.

“The Nude”

Perfectly encapsulates Lem’s feelings for Monica.

I assume Rob was singing about a statue or a painting of a nude that moved him to tears. But for me, personally, it evokes the dichotomy of seeing someone else naked — in what you would assume is their most vulnerable state — and yet you are the one who gets your heart broken.

“Fripp”

“Half Life”

This album was actually my introduction to The Catherine Wheel. “Waydown” came on the radio and blew me away.

“Heal”

I actually did not care for this track back in high school, but after the period of my life that inspired Catch Up To Myself, I really connected with it.

“Eat My Dust You Insensitive Fuck”

Contrary to the aggressive title, this is actually the most peaceful track on the album. I keep waiting for it to explode, and it never does. Love it.

“Shocking”

“Love Tips Up”

“Judy Staring at the Sun”

This sunny-sounding song is actually about the depravity of heroin addiction. But let’s not think about that, shall we? It sounds more like a summertime song to me.

“Fizzy Love”

“Kill My Soul”

This is not an official album. Rather, it is a collection of B-sides, covers, and alternative versions of previously-released material.

“Heal 2”

Just when I’d learned to love the original version of “Heal” on the Happy Days album, I discovered this alternative version. The first 75% of the song sounds the same; then it suddenly takes a different turn. I found this jarring at first, but then it, too, grew on me, and now I’d be hard-pressed to say which version I prefer.

“Wish You Were Here”

Amazing Pink Floyd cover. Brings tears to Lem’s eyes after the breakup.

Side Note: The Catherine Wheel used Storm Thorgerson’s artwork for their album covers, just like Pink Floyd.

“Backwards Guitar”

“Tongue Twisted”

“High Heels”

I love the line, “I’m a bad decision-maker.” This is how Lem feels for much of the book.

I had this album in mind for Lem’s drive out to the summer beach house.

“Intro”

“Future Boy”

“Delicious”

“Broken Nose”

An amazing song about dropping all your emotional baggage and bad habits, and moving forward in life. Which is exactly the tipping point Lem is on the brink of during his drive to the beach house.

“Ma Solituda”

“Goodbye”

This one’s a heartbreaker.

“Outro”

Another heartbreaker.

The Catherine Wheel’s final album.

“Sparks Are Gonna Fly”

That amazing, energetic feeling of new love! In fact, if you can find the radio edit, I think that version’s even punchier.

“Lifeline”

“Crème Caramel”
